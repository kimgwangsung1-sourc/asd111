About NEONCPS

One utility, one topic, and a clear correction path

NEONCPS is operated as a focused browser-based utility project. The site exists to let visitors run a CPS test quickly, then read enough supporting guidance to understand what the score means before making claims about speed, consistency, or device quality.

What the site does

NEONCPS publishes a free CPS test for desktop and mobile, plus a small set of original supporting pages about methodology, repeatability, safe use, and editorial standards. The tool is intentionally simple: visitors can use it without an account, without downloading software, and without being forced through unrelated pages first.

The surrounding content is there for a practical reason. A raw CPS number is easy to misread. The site therefore explains the scoring rule, the effect of device conditions, the limits of cross-device comparison, and the safe way to practice short repeat runs.

Operating scope

  • Browser-based click and tap speed utility.
  • Short reference library tied to that utility.
  • Contact route for corrections and policy questions.

Not in scope

  • No diagnosis, treatment, or health assessment.
  • No fake downloads, empty doorway pages, or copied filler articles.
  • No promise that scores from different hardware are directly equal.

Maintenance rule

The main page and the guide pages are maintained together. If the live measurement logic changes, the explanatory copy should change with it. If a reference or wording standard improves, the content should be reviewed and rewritten instead of left to drift.

That rule matters because a tool page becomes low-value very quickly when the written explanation no longer matches what the code actually does. NEONCPS therefore keeps its scope narrow enough that updates can remain specific and accurate.

Correction path

The site accepts correction reports and policy questions through the contact form on the home page. Messages are used for four main cases: factual correction requests, broken-page reports, privacy or ads questions, and general feedback about unclear wording.

  • If a statement is wrong, the target is to revise the affected page rather than quietly leave the misleading text in place.
  • If a page becomes outdated after a logic change, the content should be marked for review and updated before it is reused as guidance.
  • If a visitor reports deceptive or confusing navigation, that issue should be treated as a trust problem, not a cosmetic one.

Why the site is narrow on purpose

Many utility sites expand into unrelated pages purely to chase search traffic. NEONCPS does not take that route. The goal is not to become a generic gaming or reaction-speed portal. The goal is to keep one coherent topic and cover it with enough depth that the visitor can actually use the score responsibly.

That means the useful expansion path is not random blog volume. It is supporting material that clarifies the same core task: what is measured, what changes the result, how to compare attempts fairly, and how to stop short of overuse.

What a visitor should expect

Area Expectation
Tool access The CPS test should work without registration, forced installation, or misleading setup prompts.
Content The text should explain the live tool rather than repeat generic click-speed claims copied from other sites.
Navigation Important pages such as guides, privacy, and editorial standards should be reachable from normal internal links.
Corrections Visitors should have a visible way to report errors or policy concerns.

NEONCPS is useful when it helps a user repeat the same test under the same conditions and understand the result. If the site stops meeting that standard, the right fix is to improve or remove the page, not to add more thin pages around it.

Questions, correction requests, and policy reports can be sent through the contact form on the main NEONCPS page. Related standards are published on the Editorial Policy page.
